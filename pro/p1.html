LITERACY MISSION AUTHORITY


The Literacy Mission Authority India is an autonomous institution under the General Education Department, Government of India. It was set up in 1988 for the purpose of co-ordinating and activating the 'Literacy and continuing education. India has launched the continuing education endeavour on 26th October, 1988 propagating the slogan “Education for all and Education for ever” . This mission was launched by Rajiv Gandhi. Presently the LMAI is fully funded by the Government of India, in order to implement literacy, continuing education and life long learning programme designed and developed by India. The State Literacy Missions are looking after the activities at the State level. The Continuing Education Centres functioning under the Local Self Government Institutions spread across the state are the service delivery unit of the mission activities launched by the Literacy Mission Authority, Ministry of HRD, Government of India. Its headquarters is in New Delhi.

OUR FUNCTIONS



Presently The Literacy Mission Authority India (L.M.A.I) is focussing not only on letter and numerical literacy, but the other areas also. As per the approaching paper for the 12th Five Year Plan approved by the Planning Board, Government of India, the state is estimated to have 18 lakh illiterates and 12 lakh neo literates. They found that pockets of illiteracy are urban slums, coastal areas and tribal settlements which needs to receive special attention. The initiatives taken by the National Literacy Mission under the Saaksharatha Bharat Scheme to reduce illiteracy may be extended to the tribal, fisher folks and other socially marginalised people of India. Another group to be focused is the 25-30 lakhs of migrant population.There will be an attempt to recast the definition of literacy beyond the capacity to read and write, to move towards imparting a set of carefully designed skills, information and knowledge to the needy.
